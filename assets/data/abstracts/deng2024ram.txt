This paper presents RAM-Avatar, a method for creating real-time photo-realistic avatars from monocular videos with full-body control. Given a monocular video of a human performer, our method reconstructs a high-fidelity digital avatar that can be animated in real-time with full-body control including body pose, hand gesture, and facial expression. To achieve photo-realistic rendering quality and real-time performance simultaneously, we propose to combine the advantages of both mesh-based and volumetric representations. Specifically, we first reconstruct the geometry of the performer using a parametric body model and then learn an appearance model based on 3D Gaussian Splatting that is attached to the mesh surface. This hybrid representation enables efficient rendering at over 30 FPS while maintaining high visual quality. We further propose a pose-dependent appearance model that captures pose-dependent clothing deformations and shadows. Extensive experiments demonstrate that our method achieves state-of-the-art rendering quality while running in real-time, enabling various applications such as telepresence and virtual try-on.
