We present HRM^2Avatar, a framework for creating high-fidelity avatars from monocular phone scans, which can be rendered and animated in real time on mobile devices. Monocular capture with smartphones provides a low-cost alternative to studio-grade multi-view systems, making avatar digitization more accessible. However, this comes with challenges including limited capture views and dynamic deformations that are difficult to resolve. To faithfully reproduce user appearance, we propose a clothed mesh-driven Gaussian representation and a static-dynamic co-optimization strategy that jointly optimizes shape, appearance, and pose-dependent deformations, capturing fine geometric details and illumination-aware attributes. For efficient on-device rendering, we design a set of practical GPU-oriented techniques, including chunk-based Gaussian compression, hierarchical mesh-to-Gaussian culling, and single-pass stereo rendering, enabling high-fidelity display on resource-constrained mobile hardware. The resulting system delivers 120 FPS on mobile devices and 90 FPS on standalone VR devices at 2K resolution, over 2.7x faster than representative mobile-engine baselines.
